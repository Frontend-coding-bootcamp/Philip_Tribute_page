Mary Slessor - A missionary who stopped the killing of twins

Here's a timeline of Mary Slessor life;

  • 1848 - born in Aberdeen, Scotland UK
  • 1859 - worked for Baxter Brother's Mill
  • 1862 - worked as a skilled jute worker
  • 1875 - decided to follow in the footsteps of David Livingstone
  • 1876 - sailed to West Africa with her relation Robert Beedie
  • 1879 - she left Calabar for Dundee to recover after been hit with malaria
  • 1888 - she travelled north to Okoyong
  • 1892 - she became vice-consul in Okoyong
  • 1905 - she was named vice-president of Ikot Obong native court
  • 1913 - she was awarded the Order of St John
  • 1915 - died at the age of 66

During her third mission to Calabar, Slessor received news that her mother and sister had died. She was overcome with loneliness, writing “There is no one to write and tell my stories and nonsense to”. She had also found a sense of independence, writing “Heaven is now nearer to me than Britain, and no one will worry about me if I go up country”.
